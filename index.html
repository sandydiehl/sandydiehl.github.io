Research

I work on moral, social, and political philosophy, and have a particular interest in questions and themes that derive from post-Kantian European philosophy. My dissertation is about the role of mutual recognition in morality and love.

My paper "Why Immanent Critique?" is forthcoming in the European Journal of Philosophy.

A two-week course for high school students about moral and political questions raised by artificial intelligence. Topics include democracy and the flow of information, data collection and the value of privacy, fairness in algorithmic decisionmaking, and automation and work.
